A RICH HISTORY

Kandy is the last resting place of Gautama Buddha's Sacred Tooth Relic. During the reign of King Keerthi Sri Meghavarna, Princess Hemamala and Prince Dantha conveyed the sacred relic to Sri Lanka from the city of Kalinga in ancient India (Kithsirimevan 301 -328).The king was responsible for protecting the tooth relic, and it was believed that whomever gained possession of it would have the right and ability to rule Ancient Sri Lanka.It was a hallmark of Sri Lankan rulers and was carefully kept in an exceptional temple established within the royal palace grounds, wherever the capital was situated. Ruins of similar structures may still be seen at the ancient capitals of Anuradapura, Pollonnaruwa, Dambadeniya, Yapahuwa, Kurunegala, Kotte, and Gampola. While in Kandy, the last Kingdom, the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic remains to be the most revered shrine in the Buddhist world.


DESCRIPTION

Intricate sculptures in gold, silver, bronze, and ivory embellish the temple. Encircled by a lake and a brick wall known as the Wall of Water Waves or the Cloud Wall, which is also a unique architectural element of the Temple of the Tooth. The wall and moat both were constructed during King Sri Wickrama Rajasinghe's reign. The decorative windows on the wall are intended to ignite lamps at the appropriate times. The insides of the temple are decorated with extremely detailed and ornate murals, displaying Kandyan artistic elements.
Aside from these decorations, the wooden towers are another architectural highlight of the Temple of the Tooth. These have forms that are distinctive to the Kandyan period and are fashioned with center ridges. The tower's pillars are adorned with numerous engravings. The interior of the temple has been enhanced with low curving ceilings of corridors and laid flooring with polished stones.


HOW TO GET HERE?

If you are a local guest, you may take the rail, bus, or cab to Kandy. The entrance of Sri Dalada Maligawa may be seen after a large round of Kandy lake.
If you are flying into Colombo Bandaranayake International Airport, use the Katunayaka express highway until the Kadwatha exit, then take the direct route to Kandy. Alternatively, after landing at Colombo Bandaranaike International Airport, drive to Kandy from Kurunegala via Mathale.

SOME TIPS

As this is a religious place, you will have to follow a dress code.


  1. Go barefoot
  2. No shoulders visible
  3. No hats or caps
  4. No kneecaps visible
